Study in USA from Nepal
The United States is home to the largest and most prestigious higher education system in the world. With a wide array of programs and a culturally diverse environment, studying in the USA offers international students unparalleled opportunities for personal and academic growth.
Learn More

Why study in USA?

  • The USA boasts numerous top-ranked universities and colleges known for their rigorous academic programs and research opportunities.
  • Many institutions offer courses in English, providing a comfortable learning environment for non-native speakers.
  • The diversity of the American education system means students can choose from a variety of programs and specialties tailored to their interests.
  • The USA provides numerous scholarship opportunities for international students, helping to reduce the overall cost of education.
  • Graduates from US institutions are highly sought after by employers worldwide, enhancing career prospects significantly.
  • The emphasis on innovation and critical thinking in American universities prepares students for success in a global job market.
  • The rich cultural experiences available throughout the country enhance the overall educational experience.

Study in USA : Step By Step Process

  1. Research and shortlist your desired course and university.
  2. Prepare academic documents and take English proficiency tests (IELTS/PTE).
  3. Submit your application and wait for the offer letter.
  4. Arrange funds and pay initial fees to receive the Form I-20.
  5. Complete medical checks and acquire health insurance.
  6. Submit your visa application and await approval.
  7. Prepare for departure: accommodation, flights, and orientation.
Abroad Bridge Consultancy assists you at every stage—from selecting the right course to visa approval.

Scholarships, Rights & Post-Study Work

The USA offers a variety of scholarships for international students, including both need-based and merit-based options.
Students can work part-time while studying (usually up to 20 hours per week during the academic term).
Post-study work options such as the Optional Practical Training (OPT) program allow international students to work in their field of study for up to 12 months after graduation, with the possibility of extending for STEM graduates.
