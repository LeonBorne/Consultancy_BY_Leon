Study in Germany from Nepal
Germany is a top destination for international students, renowned for its high-quality education, innovative research, and strong economy. With a wide variety of programs available in English and a rich cultural experience, studying in Germany offers both academic and personal growth.
Learn More

Why study in Germany?

  • German universities are known for their excellent academic standards, with many ranked among the best in the world.
  • Many programs are available in English, making higher education accessible to international students.
  • Germany offers low or no tuition fees in public universities, making it a cost-effective choice for students.
  • The country is a hub for engineering, technology, and sciences, offering numerous research opportunities.
  • Students benefit from a diverse, multicultural environment, which fosters an inclusive atmosphere.
  • Germany’s strong economy provides ample internship and job opportunities for students, both during and after their studies.
  • The rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and vibrant cities enhance the overall student experience.

English Requirements to Study in Germany

Level Academic IELTS / PTE (typical)
Bachelor Minimum GPA 2.0 (varies by university & course) IELTS 6.0 (no band < 5.5) / PTE 50
Master Minimum GPA 2.5 with relevant background IELTS 6.5 (no band < 6.0) / PTE 55 (no band < 52)
⚠️ Requirements vary depending on the university and program. Always verify with the official provider. Check our IELTS/PTE Preparation page for more info.

Study in Germany : Step By Step Process

  1. Research and shortlist your desired course and university.
  2. Prepare academic documents and take English proficiency tests (IELTS/PTE).
  3. Submit your application and wait for the admission decision.
  4. Arrange funds and pay initial fees to secure your place.
  5. Complete health insurance requirements.
  6. Submit your visa application and await approval.
  7. Prepare for departure: accommodation, flights, and orientation.
Abroad Bridge Consultancy assists you at every stage—from selecting the right course to visa approval.

Scholarships, Rights & Post-Study Work

Germany offers various scholarships for international students, including DAAD scholarships and university-specific awards.
Students are allowed to work part-time while studying (typically up to 120 full days or 240 half days per year).
Post-study work options allow students to stay in Germany for up to 18 months to seek employment related to their degree after graduation.
