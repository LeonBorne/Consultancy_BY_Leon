Study in France from Nepal
France is renowned for its rich history, culture, and strong educational system, making it an ideal destination for international students. With a diverse range of programs and institutions, studying in France provides a unique opportunity to experience European culture while receiving a world-class education.
Learn More

Why study in France?

  • France boasts some of the top-ranked universities and grandes écoles in Europe, known for their rigorous academic standards and innovative approaches to education.
  • Many programs are available in English, allowing international students to study without needing full proficiency in French.
  • The country offers a vibrant cultural scene, historical landmarks, and diverse cuisine that enrich the student experience.
  • Tuition fees in France are relatively low compared to other Western countries, and many scholarships are available for international students.
  • French degrees are highly respected globally, enhancing employability and career opportunities.
  • The focus on research and creativity equips students with skills applicable in various industries.
  • France’s multicultural environment fosters a welcoming atmosphere for students from all over the world.

Study in France : Step By Step Process

  1. Research and shortlist your desired course and university.
  2. Prepare academic documents and take English proficiency tests (IELTS/PTE).
  3. Submit your application and wait for the offer letter.
  4. Arrange funds and pay initial fees to receive a proof of enrollment.
  5. Complete medical checks and acquire health insurance.
  6. Submit your visa application and await approval.
  7. Prepare for departure: accommodation, flights, and orientation.
Abroad Bridge Consultancy supports you every step of the way—from selecting the right course to securing your visa.

Scholarships, Rights & Post-Study Work

Various scholarships are available for international students, including merit-based and government-funded options.
International students are allowed to work part-time while studying (up to 964 hours per year).
Post-study work options are available, allowing graduates to apply for a Temporary Residence Permit (APS) for up to one year.
